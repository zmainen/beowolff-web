How it works

What is this?

Every dot is an artwork — nearly 690,000 works from the Rijksmuseum, WikiArt, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, and the National Gallery of Art. Works that are similar land near each other. The map is computed by projecting high-dimensional representations of each work into two dimensions using UMAP.

Two ways of seeing

Semantic (SigLIP) organizes by what works depict and what tradition they belong to. SigLIP is a vision-language model (Google, 2023) trained on billions of image-text pairs. It learned to encode both what things look like and what they mean — a Vermeer interior and a Hopper interior share a direction (dimly lit room, solitary figure) while differing along others (17th-century Dutch vs. 20th-century American). This is why prints, photographs, paintings, and drawings form distinct continents.

Aesthetic (Iigaya) organizes by how works feel. Thirteen perceptual dimensions — brightness, hue contrast, sharpness, color temperature, compositional balance, visual dynamics — derived from a computational model of aesthetic preference (Iigaya et al., Nature Human Behaviour 2021). A dark moody photograph and a dark moody painting land near each other regardless of medium, because they share the same aesthetic character.
